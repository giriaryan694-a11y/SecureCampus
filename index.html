‚ö†Ô∏è Why Security Matters for Schools & Colleges

Educational institutions store highly sensitive data, including:

  • Government-issued IDs (Aadhaar, passports, voter IDs).
  • Financial records (fee payments, scholarships, bank details).
  • Health records (medical histories, disability accommodations).
  • Academic records (grades, disciplinary actions).
  • Biometric data (fingerprints, facial recognition).

Real-world risks:

  • Identity theft: Stolen IDs can be used for loans, fraud, or criminal impersonation.
  • Financial fraud: Fee payment systems are prime targets for phishing/scams.
  • Blackmail: Health/academic records can be weaponized.
  • State-level threats: Foreign actors may target student data for espionage.

Legal obligations: Schools are bound by data protection laws (e.g., GDPR, India‚Äôs DPDP Act).

Step 1: Reverse Image Search (Check if a Photo is Stolen)

What it does: Finds where else a photo appears online. If a profile picture shows up on 10 different names, it‚Äôs likely fake.

How to do it (on phone/computer):

  1. Go to Google Images.
  2. Click the camera icon in the search bar.
  3. Upload the photo or paste the image URL.
  4. Check the results:
    • ‚úÖ Safe: Photo only appears on the person‚Äôs real profiles.
    • ‚ùå Fake: Photo appears on multiple names or stock photo sites.

Example: If a new teacher‚Äôs profile photo appears on a modeling website, their ID might be fake.

Step 2: Face Search (Find Other Profiles of the Same Person)

What it does: Helps you find other social media accounts of the same person.

How to do it:

  1. Use PimEyes or Social Catfish.
  2. Upload the person‚Äôs photo.
  3. Check if the face matches other profiles:
    • ‚úÖ Real: Face matches a LinkedIn/Facebook profile with the same name.
    • ‚ùå Fake: Face matches profiles with different names.

Step 3: Search Username Across Social Media

What it does: Checks if a person exists on multiple platforms (e.g., Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn).

How to do it:

  1. Go to UserSearch or WhatsMyName.
  2. Enter the person‚Äôs username (e.g., @johndoe).

Step 4: Check for Common Fake ID Signs

Red flags in IDs/profiles:

  • No posts or friends (new account).

üìç Safe Meetups Guide

üåç Online Safety

  • Use the verification steps above before trusting someone online.
  • Never share passwords, Aadhaar, or bank details.

üìç Offline Safety

Never meet at home or school. Instead, choose:

  • Unknown marts near police stations: Public and safe.
  • Clinics/hospitals: Most allow entry even without an appointment.

Pro Tips:

  • Bring a parent or trusted adult.
  • Share your live location with family.

üö® Physical Access Risks

Common vulnerabilities in schools/colleges:

  • Washroom Request: Indian law allows public washroom use. Guards rarely question this.
  • Fake Meetings: Claiming a staff meeting can grant access (avoid real names/photos).
